▪ Non-negotiable rules
• Weigh yourself weekly — morning, post-toilet, before eating. Track a 4-week average, not day-to-day.[cite: 2]

• Protein first — eat your eggs/sabji before you touch the roti or rice.[cite: 2]

• Every 4 weeks = deload — swim 50% volume, skip one lift session.[cite: 2]

• Sleep 7–8 hours — this is where fat loss and muscle gain actually happen.[cite: 2]

• 10,000 steps — walk during calls, use stairs, brief walks after lunch.[cite: 2]

• Take photos every 4 weeks — scale can stay flat while you visibly change shape.[cite: 2]
▪ When the scale stalls
• 2–3 weeks: Isn't a plateau (water retention/period). Wait it out.[cite: 2]

• 4+ weeks: Check calories first. Track everything for one week.[cite: 2]

• If genuinely stalled: Add 20 min walk after dinner. Don't cut more food yet.[cite: 2]
▪ Menstrual cycle
• Week before: 1–2 kg water weight gain is normal. Don't restrict more. Add 1 roti if craving.[cite: 2]

• During: Replace one swim with an easy 30 min walk if energy tanks.[cite: 2]

• Post-period (days 1–14): Power window. Hit your hardest sessions here.[cite: 2]
